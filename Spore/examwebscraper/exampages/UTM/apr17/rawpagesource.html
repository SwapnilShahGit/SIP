Exam Rule Reminder

AT A UNIVERSITY OF TORONTO MISSISSAUGA EXAM, IT'S NOT JUST A CELL PHONE, IT'S AN UNAUTHORIZED AID.

Unless otherwise stated, devices of any kind are not allowed at University of Toronto Mississauga exams - posession is punishable under the Code of Behaviour on Academic Matters. See section B.I.1.(b).

  1. Clear, sealable, plastic bags will be provided for all electronic devices with storage, including but not limited to: cell phones, SMART devices, tablets, laptops, calculators, and MP3 players. Students are asked to turn off all devices, seal them in the bag provided, and place the bag under their desk for the duration of the examination. They will not be able to touch the bag or its contents until the exam is over.
  2. Bags, purses, books and ALL notes are to be deposited in areas designated by the Chief Presiding Officer and are not to be taken to the examination desk or table.
  3. Candidates must not communicate with one another in any manner whatsoever during the examination.
  4. Candidates may not leave the examination room unescorted for any reason, including visiting the washroom.
  5. All writing must cease at the end of the examination.

Please see the Rules for the Conduct of Examinations for a complete list of University of Toronto Mississauga examination regulations.

Failure to follow any of these rules could result in a formal allegation of academic misconduct. Please see the Code of Behaviour on Academic Matters for more information.

Bringing an electronic device to the exams?

LATE STUDENTS:

  • If you are less than 30 minutes late, enter the examination room and begin writing. You will not receive additional time.
  • If you are more than 30 minutes late, you must report immediately to the Office of the Registrar (Room 1235, Innovation Complex Building). If it is after regular office hours, the door beside the elevator (1289K) will be open and there will be a staff member on duty to assist late students.

Reporting Conflicts

The university makes every effort to prevent the scheduling of overlapping final exams and three consecutive time slots however occasional conflicts are unavoidable.

U of T Mississauga students who have:

  1. two University of Toronto final examinations in the same time slot, or
  2. three examinations on one day, or
  3. three consecutive time slots, e.g., 1pm, 5pm, 9am OR 5pm, 9am, 1pm

should report the conflict(s) online no later than March 20, 2017

If you have a conflict between a final exam and a term test, your final exam takes precedence over the term test. You must notify the Department offering the conflicting term test to arrange an alternative time for the term test to be written.

Religious Accommodation

Requests for religious accommodation during examinations MUST be submitted online no later than March 20, 2017.

Special Consideration

Students who make personal commitments during the examination period do so at their own risk. No petitions will be accepted for special or deferred examinations to accommodate vacation, personal plans or employment obligations. The examination period is published in the Calendar and students are expected to be available throughout this period.

In case of illness or duress at the time of an exam, an affected student should consider not writing the exam and should immediately seek medical attention, obtain appropriate documentation and petition to defer the examination. NOTE: STUDENTS CANNOT RE-WRITE AN EXAMINATION THAT THEY HAVE ALREADY ATTEMPTED.

Students who cannot write a final examination due to illness or other serious causes must declare absence on ROSI on the day of the examination (or by the day after at the latest). Students must also file an online petition within 72 hours of the missed examination. Original supporting documentation must be submitted to the Office of the Registrar along with payment of the deferred exam fee within 72 hours of the missed exam. Late petitions will NOT be considered. There is a non-refundable fee of $70 for each deferred examination petition approved.

If illness is cited as the reason for a deferred exam request, a Verification of Student Illness or Injury form must show that you were examined and diagnosed at the time of illness and on the date of the exam, or by the day after at the latest.

IMPORTANT: Due to an increase in the use of forged documentation, the Office of the Registrar now verifies the authenticity of ALL medical certificates by contacting medical clinics. Students who submit forged documentation are subject to severe academic penalties.

STUDENTS WHO MISS AN DECEMBER FINAL EXAMINATION AND ARE APPROVED TO WRITE A DEFERRED EXAMINATION WILL BE SCHEDULED TO WRITE THE DEFERRED EXAM DURING APRIL 21 - 28, 2017. There will be only one opportunity to write a deferred exam. Petitions for second deferrals will only be considered in extreme circumstances - for example, if a student suffers a severe personal emergency or requires hospitilization.

Bring Your Student Card

All students are required to bring two pieces of photo ID to each exam. One MUST be a valid U of T student ID card (TCard) and the other must be government-issued (eg: driver's licence, passport). TCards will be scanned to confirm attendance at the exam. If you have lost your TCard, a replacement is available (for a fee of $12) from the TCard Office in the CCT Building at Lower Level, CCT Atrium.

It is your responsibility to be aware of the Rules of Conduct for U of T Mississauga examinations.
